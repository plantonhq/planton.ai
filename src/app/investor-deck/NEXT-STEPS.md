## Critical Reminders

### During Fundraising

**DO**:
- ‚úÖ Be honest about traction and challenges
- ‚úÖ Show passion and conviction
- ‚úÖ Ask investors for feedback even if they pass
- ‚úÖ Follow up within 24 hours after every meeting
- ‚úÖ Keep building product (don't stop shipping!)

**DON'T**:
- ‚ùå Lie or exaggerate numbers
- ‚ùå Bash competitors
- ‚ùå Take rejection personally
- ‚ùå Stop talking to customers
- ‚ùå Put all your focus on fundraising (keep building!)

### Red Flags to Watch For

**Good investors**:
- Ask thoughtful questions about product and market
- Introduce you to their portfolio companies
- Give clear timeline for decision
- Provide helpful feedback even if passing

**Bad investors**:
- Ask for free consulting ("can you analyze our portfolio company?")
- Drag out process indefinitely
- Ask for exclusivity without term sheet
- Don't return calls/emails

- **Plan A**: Extend runway by cutting personal salary

### If Multiple Investors Want In
- **Plan A**: Take money from best value-add investor
- **Plan B**: Increase round size slightly ($500K ‚Üí $750K)
- **Plan C**: Keep list of interested investors for Series A

### If Investors Want More Traction
- **Plan A**: Set milestone (e.g., "$5K MRR") and re-engage
- **Plan B**: Show growth trajectory (3 months of MoM data)
- **Plan C**: Land 1-2 notable enterprise pilots as proof

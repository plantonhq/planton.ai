## If You Get a "Yes"

### When Investor Shows Strong Interest

**Immediate actions**:
1. Express enthusiasm (but stay professional)
2. Ask about their process and timeline
3. Provide any additional materials they request
4. Introduce to existing customers for reference checks
5. Be responsive (reply within hours, not days)

**Due diligence prep**:
- Financial model ready
- Customer references lined up
- Technical architecture doc ready
- Legal docs clean (incorporation, IP, etc.)

---

## If You Get a "No"

### When Investor Passes

**Immediate actions**:
1. Thank them for their time
2. Ask for specific feedback: "What made this a pass?"
3. Ask if it could be a fit later: "What milestones would change your mind?"
4. Request intros: "Know anyone who might be interested?"
5. Stay on good terms (they might invest in Series A)

**Learn and iterate**:
- Track reasons for passes
- If 3+ investors say same thing, update deck
- Don't take it personally (most startups get 90%+ rejections)

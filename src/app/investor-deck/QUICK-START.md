## 5. Prepare 3 Answers (5 minutes)

You WILL get asked these. Know your answers:

### Q1: "Why solo founder?"
**Answer**: "I started solo to move fast. I've proven I can ship. Post-funding, I'm hiring immediately - ML engineer, growth lead, senior devs. I have candidates ready. Not staying solo."

### Q2: "How will you compete with Terraform?"
**Answer**: "Different value prop. Terraform is for infrastructure experts. We're for developers who don't want to become Terraform experts. We abstract the complexity, consistent experience across clouds."

### Q3: "What's your unfair advantage?"
**Answer**: "Four things: One, founder-market fit - I lived the pain. Two, 150-component head start - 2 years of work. Three, open-source community building trust. Four, capital efficiency from India."

## 8. During the Meeting

### First 2 Minutes
- Smile, introduce yourself warmly
- Ask: "How much time do we have?" (adjust pitch accordingly)
- Brief context: "Thanks for taking the time. I'm excited to show you Planton Cloud."

### During Pitch
- Deliver opening from memory (don't read slides)
- Go through deck at steady pace (not rushed)
- Pause for questions (don't steamroll)
- Read the room (engaged? bored? confused?)
- Show demo if they want to see product

### Handling Questions
- Listen fully before answering
- Pause to think if needed (shows thoughtfulness)
- Answer directly and concisely
- If you don't know: "Great question. I don't have that data right now, but I'll get it to you by tomorrow."

### Final 2 Minutes
- Summarize key points quickly
- Make the ask clearly
- Propose next steps: "Would you be open to a follow-up call next week to discuss further?"
- Thank them for their time

## If You're Nervous (Totally Normal!)

### Remember:

**They're human too**: Investors are just people. They want to find great companies to invest in. You might be that company.

**You have leverage**: You're offering them an opportunity, not begging. Good investors know great founders are rare.

**Rejection is normal**: Even the best startups get rejected by 90%+ of investors. It's a numbers game.

**You're prepared**: You have a great deck, a working product, real traction. You know your stuff.

**Worst case**: They say no. That's it. You learn and pitch the next investor.

**Best case**: They say yes. You get capital to build the next Postman.

## Emergency Tips

### If Demo Breaks
"Let me show you a screen recording instead - I have it prepared."

### If You Blank on a Number
"I don't have that exact number in my head, but I can get it to you today."

### If They Ask Something You Don't Know
"That's a great question I haven't thought deeply about. Let me research that and get back to you."

### If They Seem Uninterested
"I want to make sure I'm covering what's most relevant to you - what would be most valuable for me to focus on?"

### If You're Running Out of Time
"I see we're short on time. Let me jump to the key points..." [Go to slides 6, 11, 12]
